ASD vs ASD

9.17.2019

I was having a discussion with a co-worker today and the question came up about the difference between ASD and LRFD specifically with reference to the AISC 14th Ed. I thought I knew the answer but when I tried to explain it became readily apparent to me that I did not so I stopped trying to explain something I didn't understand.

But I couldn't just let it go so to the Google machine I went to find answers. Ref 1 Ref 2 Ref 3

So ASD stands for Allowable Stress Design and for Allowable Strength Design depending on what you are talking about. The capacity equations for Allowable Stress Design are actually different than LRFD. Whereas, the capacity equations for Allowable Strength Design are basically the same as LRFD. Also Allowable Stress Design was based on stress below the elastic limit which is not necessarily the case in Allowable Strength Design/LRFD. Allowable Strength Design is basically LRFD with "safety factors" instead of load and resistance factors.

Using the same acrynom for two seemingly similar but different design philosphies is confusing, who knew? /s
